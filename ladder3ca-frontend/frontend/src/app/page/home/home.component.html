Welcome to the LadderWeb a machine-learning based assistant for pragmatic annotation.

LadderWeb is a web application designed to improve the way researchers, teachers and learners deal with speech acts. The application provides pragmatic annotations for German and Italian cancellations and requests, thus simplifying and enhancing the analysis of pragmatic elements in L1 and L2.

Empowering Pragmatic Annotation

LadderWeb introduces an innovative approach for annotating speech acts and transforms a traditionally manual and time-consuming process into an efficient and accessible task. Users can annotate refusal or request messages in German and Italian more easily, and new potentials in corpus pragmatics and language teaching are unlocked. This functionality is available to all users and facilitates the engagement with linguistic data.

Advanced Features for Registered Users

As a registered user, you gain access to an array of advanced features, including the ability to explore, visualize, and export data. LadderWeb’s integration with OAI-PMH APIs streamlines the process of data harvesting and offers invaluable resources for creating (self-)learning materials and for enhancing pre-service teacher training. It also supports in-service educators and students in studying native and foreign languages through the analysis of elicited data, following a data-driven learning approach.

Flexible and Adaptive Usage

LadderWeb is not just a static tool but a flexible instrument that allows for the reuse of data and the adaptation of models to meet new requirements. Whether integrating data from additional languages, applying different annotation schemas, or exploring further speech acts, LadderWeb’s model is designed to evolve according to the community’s needs.

Addressing a Critical Gap

As a groundbreaking prototype, LadderWeb addresses a significant gap in corpus-based pragmatic research, particularly for Italian and German which are not as well investigated languages as English. It was highlighted by Weisser in 2018 that the lack of adequately annotated, publicly available corpora has constrained the exploration of linguistic interactions and the application of practical language training. LadderWeb steps in to fill this void by assisting researchers in pragmatic annotation.
